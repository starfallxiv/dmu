## Quick role split

After Kefka summons **Chaos** and **Exdeath**, the debuffs that split the group into light parties are assigned based on distance from the boss.

| Side | Players |
|---|---|
| **Chaos side** | ST, H2, D1, D2 |
| **Exdeath side** | MT, H1, D3, D4 |

This specific split is used because of tank invuln routing.

## Bawl of Agony — normal/legit explanation / 正攻法

After the raidwide, players receive **Chaos Fire**, **Chaos Water**, or **Chaos Wind**. Fire, Water, and Wind crystals spawn on numbered waymarks.

The crystal layout rule:

| Rule | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Fire and Water are opposite each other | They always spawn on opposite sides. |
| Wind is between Fire and Water | Wind is on one side between them. |
| Opposite Wind has no crystal | The fourth direction is empty. |
| Fire/Water can be reversed | The setup may mirror. |

### Debuff and crystal cheat sheet

| Item | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Chaos Wind** | Cleansed by taking knockback. There are front-hit and back-hit versions. |
| **Chaos Fire** | Two players get it. When it expires, they drop point-blank AoEs. |
| **Chaos Water** | Two players get it. When it expires, they drop donut AoEs. |
| **Fire crystal** | After Fire expires, the crystal gives nearby players donut AoEs. Water players use this to cleanse Wind. |
| **Water crystal** | After Water expires, the crystal gives nearby players point-blank AoEs. Fire players use this to cleanse Wind. |
| **Wind crystal** | After Wind is cleansed, it later does a two-person shared hit on nearby players. |

Fire/Water debuffs always go to **2 support players** and **2 DPS players**.

Priority for Fire/Water crystal positions, from inner to outer:

```text
Tank > Healer > D1 > D2 > D3 > D4
```

Wind crystal bait priority for supports, from inner to outer:

```text
MT > ST > H1 > H2
```

DPS should avoid disrupting the support bait and take their assigned hits from inner to outer:

```text
D1 > D2 > D3 > D4
```

## Ultima Blaster / アルテマブラスター

During **Vacuum Wave**, watch Kefka’s dash pattern.

1. Kefka dashes once.
2. Use the landing point as the reference.
3. Watch whether the second dash goes clockwise or counterclockwise.
4. Dice players line up in the **opposite rotation** from Kefka’s second dash.

Example: if Kefka’s second dash goes counterclockwise, dice line up clockwise starting from 1.

<img src="https://yan-flash.com/api/uploads/1781174669406-92acd8d8.webp" alt="P3 Ultima Blaster — read Kefka dash, line dice opposite" style="max-width: 480px; height: auto;">



The long arrow indicates the first dash. The shorter arrow indicates the second dash.

Example macro visualization:

<img src="https://yan-flash.com/api/uploads/1781191349337-af776764.webp" alt="P3 Ultima Blaster — macro reference image" style="max-width: 480px; height: auto;">



After the dice setup, Exdeath casts Thunder III on the closest players, so tanks need to be positioned correctly.

# P3 Second Half / フェーズ3 後半

## Decisive Battle 2 / 決戦2回目

Use the same light-party split as the first Decisive Battle.

Exdeath then casts **Thunder III / サンダガ** on the nearest players, so tanks should handle proximity/distance swap cleanly.

Chaos follows with **Earthquake / じしん**, a raidwide that applies two major debuff types.

| Debuff | Handling |
|---|---|
| **Chaos Sludge / 混沌の汚泥** | Goes to one healer and one DPS. Cleanse by healing the target to full HP. Cleanse order is fixed as **Healer → DPS**. |
| **Chaos Earth / 混沌の土** | Everyone gets this. It is cleansed by dropping to 1 HP via black-hole beams. |

When Chaos Sludge is cleansed, the central Earth crystal deals raidwide damage and applies a short Earth vulnerability. Healer timing matters.

## Target markers and Black Hole beams

Every player gets a target marker from 1 to 3:

| Target marker | Count | Macro marker |
|---|---:|---|
| First Target | 3 players | Attack |
| Second Target | 3 players | Bind |
| Third Target | 2 players | Stop |

Use these markers to decide who takes the beams from Black Hole.

Black Hole line pattern:

```text
Black Hole 1: 1 line → 2 lines
Black Hole 2: 3 lines → 3 lines → 3 lines
Black Hole 3: 3 lines → 3 lines → 3 lines
Black Hole 4: 2 lines → 1 line
Total: 24 beams
```

The beam priority is taken clockwise from the giant Kefka spawn. Aim the beams clockwise.

## Dondoko Stomp / どんどこ地団駄

Treat the direction where **As I Truly Am** came from as north. In the example image, it came from marker 1, so marker 1 is treated as north.

Everyone stacks center to bait Blizzaga. When the cast resolves, identify north from giant Kefka’s feet, then split:

| Position | Players |
|---|---|
| Northwest | MT, H1 |
| Northeast | ST, H2 |
| Southwest | D1, D3 |
| Southeast | D2, D4 |

# P3 Timeline Summary

| Mechanic | What to do |
|---|---|
| Decisive Battle | Split into the same light parties as P3 first half. |
| Thunder III → Earthquake | Tanks handle proximity buster. Raidwide applies Sludge and Earth debuffs. Heal Sludge targets to full in order: healer first, then DPS. |
| Binbin Binta | Check Kefka’s raised hand. Move to the safe side and resolve stack or role spread. |
| Black Hole 1 | Attack markers begin beam handling. |
| Thunder III | Tanks handle proximity. Non-tanks away from Exdeath. |
| Binbin Binta + Damning Edict | Read Chaos half-room plus Kefka hand. |
| Black Hole 2 | Attack group handles beams, with Attack → Bind swaps. |
| Damning Edict + As I Truly Am | Read Kefka arena split plus Chaos half-room. |
| Thunder III | Tanks handle proximity. |
| Black Hole 3 | Bind group handles beams, with Bind → Stop swaps. |
| Binbin Binta + White Hole + Implosion | Full HP for White Hole. Angle Chaos at 45 degrees. |
| Black Hole 4 | Stop group handles final beams. |
| As I Truly Am | Final line player must be careful with beam direction. |
| Blizzaga + Dondoko Stomp + Landing | Read north from giant Kefka’s feet, then role 2:2 positions, stacks, and towers. |
| Empowered Blizzaga + Protrude | Spread as soon as Protrude cast appears, then keep moving. |
| Meteor + Bawl of Agony enrage | Enrage. |

## One-screen mental model

```text
P3 First Half:
Crystal/debuff cleanup → Chaos angle → Wind knockback → Kefka dash/dice

P3 Second Half:
HP-state debuffs → Black Hole beam accounting → Binta/White Hole checks → stomps/towers
```

The spicy part is not “what does my marker mean?”  
The spicy part is remembering that the beam mechanic has planned handoffs. Do not autopilot it.
